## Workout Breakdown

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Sit comfortably on the rower or ski erg, feet secured.
    - For rowing: Start with your knees bent, arms extended, and back straight. Drive through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest.
    - For skiing: Stand tall, grip the handles, and use your legs to push off while pulling the handles down to your hips.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your core engaged and back straight to avoid rounding.
    - In rowing, focus on a smooth, powerful leg drive followed by a strong pull with your arms.
    - In skiing, use your whole body, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or adjust the resistance to a lower setting.
    - Consider using a stationary bike if rowing or skiing isn’t available.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel, and bring the other foot up to meet it.
    - Step back down one foot at a time, maintaining control.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and core tight throughout the movement.
    - Ensure your entire foot is on the box before stepping down to avoid slipping.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If balance is an issue, hold onto a wall or a sturdy object for support.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, kettlebell between your feet.
    - Hinge at the hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels, extend your hips, and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping your elbows high.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Maintain a flat back and engage your core as you lift.
    - Keep the kettlebell close to your body throughout the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement or if your form is breaking down.
    - If kettlebells are unavailable, you can substitute with dumbbells or perform a sumo deadlift without the high pull.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to pull your body up comfortably.
    - Grab the rings with an overhand grip, lean back, and keep your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your elbows close to your body.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Engage your core and avoid sagging your hips.
    - Focus on pulling with your back muscles rather than just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Adjust the height of the rings to make the movement easier or harder; lower rings make it easier.
    - If rings are not available, you can use a barbell set at waist height for inverted rows.
