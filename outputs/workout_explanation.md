## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Rowing**: Sit on the rower, strap your feet in, and grab the handle. Start with your knees bent, back straight, and arms extended.
  - **Drive**: Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest.
  - **Recovery**: Extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
  
- **Ski Erg**: Stand tall, grab the handles, and start with your arms extended overhead.
  - **Pull**: Engage your core and pull the handles down towards your hips, using your legs to assist.
  - **Return**: Let the handles return to the starting position while keeping your core engaged.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back straight and avoid hunching over.
- Use your legs to generate power, not just your arms.
- Maintain a steady rhythm; don’t rush!

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If you're new to rowing or skiing, consider reducing the time to 2:00.
- Focus on technique rather than speed; it’s okay to take breaks if needed.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- Stand in front of the box with feet hip-width apart.
- Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel, and bring the other foot up to meet it on the box.
- Step back down one foot at a time, alternating legs.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your chest up and core engaged.
- Use your legs to lift, not your back.
- Ensure the entire foot is on the box to avoid slipping.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
- If balance is an issue, hold onto a wall or a sturdy object for support.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, kettlebell between your feet.
- Hinge at the hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
- Drive through your heels, extending your hips and knees, and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping elbows high.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back flat and chest up during the hinge.
- Use your legs to initiate the movement, not just your arms.
- Control the kettlebell on the way down to avoid injury.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
- If kettlebells are unavailable, you can use dumbbells or perform a bodyweight squat instead.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- Set the rings at a height where you can comfortably reach them while standing.
- Grab the rings with an overhand grip, lean back, and keep your body straight.
- Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your elbows close to your body.

#### Form Tips:
- Engage your core to maintain a straight line from head to heels.
- Avoid using momentum; focus on controlled movements.
- Keep your shoulders down and away from your ears.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If rings are too challenging, perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX.
- You can also do bent-over rows with a dumbbell if rings are unavailable.
