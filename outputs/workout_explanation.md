## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the Row: Sit on the rower with your feet secured. Grab the handle with both hands, keeping your back straight and core engaged.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - Reverse the motion: extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
    - For the Ski Erg: Stand tall, grip the handles, and initiate the movement by pulling down with your arms while driving your knees slightly forward.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Maintain a strong core throughout the movement.
    - Keep your back straight and avoid rounding your shoulders.
    - Use your legs to generate power, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or 1:30.
    - Focus on technique rather than speed; it’s okay to go slower to maintain form.

### 2. Box Step-ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box to avoid slipping.
    - Alternate legs with each rep to ensure balanced strength.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on balance and form.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes pointed slightly out.
    - Place the kettlebell between your feet. Hinge at the hips and grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body, leading with your elbows.
    - Lower the kettlebell back down to the ground in a controlled manner.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and chest up during the lift.
    - Ensure your elbows stay above your wrists as you pull the kettlebell up.
    - Engage your glutes and hamstrings to power the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell or perform the movement with just bodyweight to focus on the hinge pattern.
    - If kettlebells are not available, you can use dumbbells or perform a bodyweight deadlift.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back comfortably while holding onto them.
    - Grab the rings with an overhand grip and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings by bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
    - Lower yourself back down in a controlled manner.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Avoid letting your hips sag or your shoulders round forward.
    - Focus on pulling with your back, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If rings are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a barbell or use a TRX.
    - Adjust the angle of your body to make it easier or harder.
