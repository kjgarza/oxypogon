## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the Row: Sit on the rower with your feet secured. Grab the handle and start with your knees bent and back straight.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - Reverse the motion: extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
    - For the Ski Erg: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grasp the handles, and pull down while driving your elbows back, engaging your core.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - In the row, focus on using your legs first, then your back and arms.
    - In the Ski Erg, maintain a smooth rhythm and avoid hunching your shoulders.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing, consider reducing the time to 2:00 or using a lower resistance setting.
    - For the Ski Erg, you can also switch to a rowing machine if you’re more comfortable with that.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 Reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core as you step up.
    - Use your arms for balance by swinging them as you step.
    - Ensure your entire foot is on the box to avoid slipping.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on form.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 Reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out. Place the kettlebell between your feet.
    - Hinge at the hips and bend your knees to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body to your chin, keeping your elbows high.
    - Lower the kettlebell back down to the ground and repeat.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and chest up during the lift.
    - Use your legs to initiate the movement, not just your arms.
    - Ensure the kettlebell stays close to your body throughout the pull.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement or focus on the deadlift portion without the high pull.
    - You can also perform this with a dumbbell if kettlebells are not available.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 Reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended.
    - Grab the rings and lean back, keeping your body straight from head to heels.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings by bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
    - Lower yourself back to the starting position and repeat.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line; avoid sagging your hips or arching your back.
    - Focus on pulling with your back and arms, not just your arms.
    - Adjust the height of the rings to make the movement easier or harder.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If rings are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX.
    - Alternatively, you can do bent-over dumbbell rows for a similar effect.
