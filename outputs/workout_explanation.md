## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the rower: Sit with your feet strapped in, grab the handle with both hands, and start with your knees bent and back straight.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - Reverse the motion: extend your arms, hinge at the hips, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
    - For the Ski Erg: Stand tall, holding the handles. Initiate the movement by pulling down with your arms while driving your knees slightly forward.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your core engaged throughout the movement to protect your back.
    - Use your legs to generate power; don’t just rely on your arms.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or focus on technique rather than intensity.
    - Consider using a lower resistance setting on the Ski Erg if it feels too challenging.

### 2. Box Step-ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand in front of the box with your feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Stand tall at the top, then step back down with the same foot and repeat on the other side.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and core engaged as you step up.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box to avoid slipping.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If you need more stability, hold onto a wall or a sturdy object while stepping up.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out.
    - Place the kettlebell between your feet and hinge at the hips to grab the handle with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body, leading with your elbows.
    - Lower the kettlebell back down to the ground and repeat.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back flat and chest up during the lift.
    - Ensure your elbows stay higher than your wrists at the top of the pull.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
    - If kettlebells are not available, you can substitute with dumbbells or perform a bodyweight squat instead.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height where you can comfortably reach them while standing.
    - Grab the rings with an overhand grip and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top.
    - Lower yourself back down with control and repeat.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Avoid letting your hips sag or your shoulders round forward.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If rings are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a barbell or use a TRX.
    - Adjust the angle of your body to make it easier or harder, depending on your strength level.
