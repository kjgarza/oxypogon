## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - **Rowing:** Sit on the rower, strap your feet in, and grab the handle. Start with your knees bent and back straight. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - **Ski Erg:** Stand tall with a slight bend in your knees. Grip the handles and pull them down towards your hips while engaging your core and using your legs to drive the movement.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and avoid hunching over.
    - Use your legs to initiate the movement; your arms should follow.
    - For the Ski Erg, focus on engaging your core and using your hips to drive the motion.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you're new to rowing, consider reducing the intensity by rowing at a steady pace rather than sprinting.
    - For the Ski Erg, you can also switch to a seated row if you have access to one.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand in front of the box with your feet hip-width apart. Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first, then step back down one foot at a time.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box before stepping down to maintain balance.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re still building strength or balance.
    - You can also perform step-ups without weights or use a dumbbell for added resistance.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, kettlebell on the ground between your feet. Hinge at your hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels to lift the kettlebell, pulling it up along your body to your chin, keeping your elbows high.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back flat and chest up as you hinge.
    - Use your legs to generate power, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to the movement or focus on the deadlift portion without the high pull.
    - Alternatively, you can perform a dumbbell deadlift high pull if kettlebells are not available.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to pull your body up while keeping your feet on the ground. Grab the rings and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Avoid using momentum; focus on a controlled pull.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If ring rows are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a barbell or use a TRX.
    - Alternatively, you can do bent-over dumbbell rows if rings are not available.
