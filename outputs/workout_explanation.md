## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps**:
    - **Rowing**: Sit on the rower, strap your feet in, and grab the handle. Start with your knees bent and your back straight. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest, then return smoothly.
    - **Ski Erg**: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grip the handles, and initiate the movement by pulling down with your arms while engaging your core and legs. Keep your body upright and use your legs to drive the movement.
  
- **Form Tips**:
    - Keep your back straight and core engaged to avoid rounding your spine.
    - In rowing, focus on a smooth, powerful leg drive followed by a strong pull with your arms.
    - In skiing, maintain a steady rhythm and avoid overextending your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications**:
    - If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or even 1:00 to build confidence.
    - Focus on technique over speed; it’s better to row or ski slower with good form than to rush and risk injury.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps**:
    - Stand in front of the box with your feet hip-width apart. Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box. Bring your other foot up to meet the first, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips**:
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - Ensure your entire foot is on the box to avoid slipping and to maximize stability.

- **Scaling/Modifications**:
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If you have knee issues, consider doing step-ups without weights or using a bench.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps**:
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out. Hold the kettlebell with both hands in front of you. Hinge at your hips, keeping your back straight, and lower the kettlebell down between your legs. 
    - Explosively extend your hips and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping your elbows high and outside.
  
- **Form Tips**:
    - Focus on using your legs to drive the movement, not just your arms.
    - Keep the kettlebell close to your body throughout the pull.

- **Scaling/Modifications**:
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement or if you’re feeling fatigued.
    - If kettlebells are not available, you can substitute with dumbbells.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps**:
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended. Grab the rings with an overhand grip and walk your feet forward until your body is at an angle. Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your body straight, then lower back down.
  
- **Form Tips**:
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels; avoid sagging your hips.
    - Engage your lats and pull with your elbows rather than just your hands.

- **Scaling/Modifications**:
    - If ring rows are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a barbell or use a TRX.
    - Adjust the angle of your body to make it easier or harder depending on your strength level.
