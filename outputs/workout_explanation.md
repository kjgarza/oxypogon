## Workout Components Breakdown

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the Row: Sit on the rower with your feet secured. Start with your knees bent and arms extended. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - For the Ski Erg: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grasp the handles. Start with your arms overhead, then pull down while bending your knees slightly, engaging your core.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - In rowing, focus on driving with your legs first, then pulling with your arms.
    - In the Ski Erg, use your whole body, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing, consider reducing the time to 2:00 or using a lower resistance.
    - For the Ski Erg, you can also perform a seated row with resistance bands if equipment is limited.

### 2. Box Step-ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with feet hip-width apart. Step up with one foot, driving through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
    - Step down carefully, alternating legs with each rep.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core as you step up.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box to avoid any slips.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If you need more challenge, hold a dumbbell in each hand.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out. Hold the kettlebell with both hands in front of you.
    - Hinge at the hips and lower the kettlebell to the ground, then explosively extend your hips and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping your elbows high.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back flat and engage your glutes as you lift.
    - The kettlebell should travel close to your body throughout the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement or perform a standard deadlift instead.
    - If you don’t have a kettlebell, you can use a dumbbell.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended. Grab the rings and walk your feet forward.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your body straight, then lower back down.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Engage your lats and core throughout the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If rings are too challenging, perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX strap.
    - You can also perform bent-over dumbbell rows if rings are not available.
