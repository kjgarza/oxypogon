## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

**Technique Steps:**
- **Rowing:**
  - Start by sitting on the rower with your feet secured in the foot straps.
  - Grip the handle with both hands, arms extended, and lean slightly forward from your hips.
  - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest while keeping your elbows close.
  - Reverse the motion smoothly: extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees.
  
- **Ski Erg:**
  - Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grip the handles.
  - Start with your arms extended overhead.
  - Pull the handles down towards your hips while bending your knees slightly, engaging your core.
  - Return to the starting position with control.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your back straight and core engaged throughout the movement.
- For rowing, focus on a powerful leg drive followed by a strong pull with your arms.
- For the Ski Erg, maintain a fluid motion, using your legs to assist the pull.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or even 1:00 to build confidence.
- Focus on technique rather than speed to ensure you’re getting the most out of the movement.

### 2. Box Step-ups (20 reps)

**Technique Steps:**
- Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
- Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
- Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, standing tall.
- Step back down one foot at a time, maintaining control.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your chest up and core engaged to avoid leaning forward.
- Use a box height that allows you to maintain good form; your knee should not extend past your toes when stepping up.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- Use a lower box if you’re unsure about your balance or strength.
- If you have knee issues, consider doing step-ups without weights or using a sturdy platform.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

**Technique Steps:**
- Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out.
- Place the kettlebell between your feet and hinge at the hips to grab the handle.
- Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body, leading with your elbows.
- Lower the kettlebell back down with control to the starting position.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your back straight and chest up during the lift.
- Ensure your elbows stay above the kettlebell as you pull it up.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement or if your form starts to break down.
- You can also perform a standard kettlebell deadlift if the high pull is too challenging.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

**Technique Steps:**
- Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back comfortably.
- Grab the rings with an overhand grip and walk your feet forward until your body is at an angle.
- Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your body straight, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
- Lower yourself back down with control.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your core tight and avoid sagging your hips.
- Focus on pulling with your back rather than just your arms.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- Adjust the height of the rings to make the movement easier or harder.
- If rings are too challenging, you can substitute with TRX rows or bent-over dumbbell rows.
