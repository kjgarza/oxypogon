## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the rower, sit with your feet strapped in, grip the handle, and start with your knees bent and back straight.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - Reverse the motion: extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
    - For the Ski Erg, stand tall, grip the handles, and pull down with your arms while driving your knees up slightly, mimicking a skiing motion.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your core engaged to protect your back.
    - Use your legs to drive the movement, not just your arms.
    - Maintain a smooth, controlled rhythm.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing, focus on shorter intervals (1:00) or use a lower resistance setting.
    - For the Ski Erg, adjust the height of the handles if needed for comfort.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first, then step back down one foot at a time.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core.
    - Drive through your heel to activate your glutes.
    - Alternate legs for each rep to ensure balance.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - Perform step-ups without weights or use a plate for added challenge.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out, and the kettlebell on the ground between your feet.
    - Hinge at the hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Stand up, driving through your heels, and as you reach standing, pull the kettlebell up towards your chin, keeping your elbows high.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and chest up during the lift.
    - Use your legs to initiate the movement, not just your arms.
    - Ensure the kettlebell stays close to your body throughout the pull.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell or perform the movement without weight to focus on form.
    - If you have shoulder issues, consider a kettlebell deadlift instead.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back comfortably while holding onto the rings.
    - Start in a lean-back position, arms extended, and pull your chest towards the rings by bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
    - Lower yourself back to the starting position with control.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body straight from head to heels; avoid sagging your hips.
    - Engage your core throughout the movement.
    - Focus on pulling with your back, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Adjust the height of the rings to make the movement easier or harder.
    - If rings are not available, substitute with inverted rows on a barbell or TRX.
