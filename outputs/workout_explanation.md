## Workout Components Breakdown

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - **Rowing:** Sit on the rower with your feet secured. Start with your knees bent and arms extended. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - **Ski Erg:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding the handles. Start with your arms extended overhead. Pull down with your arms while simultaneously bending your knees slightly, engaging your core.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and core engaged throughout the movement.
    - For rowing, focus on driving with your legs first, then pulling with your arms.
    - For the Ski Erg, think about using your whole body, not just your arms.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing, aim for a slower pace to focus on technique.
    - For Ski Erg, reduce the resistance if it feels too challenging.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with feet hip-width apart. Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
    - Bring the other foot up to meet the first, then step back down one foot at a time.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and shoulders back as you step.
    - Ensure your knee doesn’t extend past your toes when stepping up.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If you have knee issues, consider doing step-ups without weights or using a platform.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out. Hold the kettlebell with both hands in front of you.
    - Hinge at your hips and lower the kettlebell between your legs. Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up to your chin, elbows high.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back flat and core engaged during the hinge.
    - The kettlebell should stay close to your body as you pull it up.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
    - If kettlebells are unavailable, you can use a dumbbell or perform a standard deadlift instead.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height where you can comfortably reach them. Grab the rings with an overhand grip and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together, then lower back down.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Avoid letting your hips sag or your shoulders round forward.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If ring rows are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a bar or use a band for assistance.
    - Alternatively, you can do bent-over dumbbell rows.
