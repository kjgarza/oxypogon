## Workout Components Breakdown

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - **Rowing:** Sit on the rower, strap your feet in, and grab the handle. Start with your knees bent and arms extended. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - **Ski Erg:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grasp the handles. Start with your arms extended overhead, then pull down while bending your knees slightly, engaging your core as you drive your arms down and back.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and core engaged.
    - For rowing, focus on a strong leg drive before pulling with your arms.
    - For the Ski Erg, use your legs to assist your arms, creating a fluid motion.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing, reduce the intensity or time to 2 minutes.
    - For Ski Erg, you can also perform a modified version by using a resistance band for upper body pulls.

### 2. Box Step-ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand in front of the box with your feet hip-width apart. Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body up onto the box. Bring the other foot up to meet it, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box before stepping down.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on form.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out. Hold the kettlebell with both hands between your legs. Hinge at the hips, keeping your back straight, and grab the kettlebell. Drive through your heels, extend your hips, and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, leading with your elbows.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep the kettlebell close to your body throughout the movement.
    - Ensure your elbows are higher than your wrists at the top of the pull.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
    - If kettlebells are not available, you can substitute with dumbbells.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended. Grab the rings, lean back, and pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your body straight.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If rings are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows using a barbell or a TRX strap.
    - Adjust the angle of your body to make it easier or harder.
