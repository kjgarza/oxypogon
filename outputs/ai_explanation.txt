## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is all about quality and consistency. We’ll be going through 6 rounds of a mix of cardio and strength movements designed to build endurance, strength, and coordination. The goal here is to maintain good form throughout each round, focusing on controlled movements rather than rushing through. Let’s break it down step by step!

---

## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Adjust the foot straps on the rower or ski erg to fit snugly around your feet.
- **Grip**: For the rower, grab the handle with both hands, keeping your arms extended. For the ski erg, hold the handles with a neutral grip.
- **Starting Position**: Sit tall, engage your core, and lean slightly forward from the hips.
- **Movement**: 
  - For the rower: Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
  - For the ski erg: Start with a slight bend in your knees, then drive your arms down while engaging your core and legs.
- **Finish**: Return to the starting position in a controlled manner.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back straight and avoid rounding your shoulders.
- Use your legs to drive the movement; your arms should follow.
- Maintain a steady pace; don’t go all out at the start.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If you're new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or even 1:30.
- Focus on technique rather than speed; it’s okay to take breaks as needed.

---

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
- **Movement**: 
  - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel.
  - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on top of the box.
  - Step back down one foot at a time, maintaining control.
  
#### Form Tips:
- Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
- Use your arms for balance; they can help propel you upward.
- Ensure your entire foot is on the box before stepping down.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lower box if you’re new to step-ups or have mobility issues.
- You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on form.

---

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out. Place the kettlebell between your feet.
- **Movement**: 
  - Hinge at the hips and bend your knees to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
  - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body.
  - As the kettlebell reaches chest height, rotate your elbows under and catch it at the top.
  
#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back flat and chest up during the hinge.
- Use your legs to initiate the lift, not just your arms.
- Keep the kettlebell close to your body throughout the movement.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
- If kettlebells are unavailable, you can substitute with dumbbells.

---

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Adjust the rings to a height where you can comfortably reach them while standing.
- **Starting Position**: Grab the rings with an overhand grip, lean back, and keep your body straight.
- **Movement**: 
  - Pull your chest towards the rings by bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  - Lower yourself back to the starting position in a controlled manner.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your body straight from head to heels; avoid sagging your hips.
- Focus on pulling with your back, not just your arms.
- Maintain a steady pace; control the movement both up and down.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If rings are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows using a barbell or a TRX.
- Adjust the angle of your body to make the movement easier or harder.

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat**: 
  - Row/Ski: 2:00
  - Box Step-Ups: 16/8 inch box
  - Kettlebell: 16/8 kg
  - Ring Rows: 8 reps

- **Train**: 
  - Row/Ski: 3:00
  - Box Step-Ups: 20 reps on a 20/12 inch box
  - Kettlebell: 20/12 kg
  - Ring Rows: 10 reps

- **Compete**: 
  - Row/Ski: 3:00
  - Box Step-Ups: 20 reps on a 24/16 inch box
  - Kettlebell: 24/16 kg
  - Ring Rows: 10 reps

### Strategy Advice:
- Focus on maintaining a consistent pace throughout the rounds. 
- Break up the movements into manageable sets if needed, especially on the kettlebell pulls and box step-ups.
- Remember, it’s about quality over quantity—keep your form sharp!
