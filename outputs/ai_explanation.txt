## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Adjust the damper setting on the rower or ski erg to a comfortable level (around 3-5 for most).
- **Grip**: For the rower, grip the handle with both hands, keeping your arms straight. For the ski erg, hold the handles with a neutral grip.
- **Start Position**: Sit tall with your back straight, feet secured, and knees slightly bent.
- **Movement**: 
  - For the rower: Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest. Reverse the motion smoothly.
  - For the ski erg: Engage your core, pull down with your arms while driving your knees up slightly, and return to the start position.
  
#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back straight and avoid rounding your shoulders.
- Use your legs to drive the movement; your arms should follow.
  
#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If youâ€™re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or switch to a stationary bike for a similar cardio effect.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Stand in front of a sturdy box or platform, feet hip-width apart.
- **Movement**: 
  - Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
  - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
#### Form Tips:
- Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
- Use your arms for balance if needed.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lower box if youâ€™re not comfortable with the height.
- Perform step-ups without weights or use a dumbbell for added resistance.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out, kettlebell on the ground between your feet.
- **Movement**: 
  - Hinge at your hips and grab the kettlebell with both hands.
  - Stand up, driving through your heels, and pull the kettlebell up towards your chin, keeping your elbows high.
  - Lower the kettlebell back down to the ground and repeat.
  
#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back flat and chest up during the hinge.
- Ensure the kettlebell stays close to your body as you pull it up.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lighter kettlebell or perform the movement without weight to focus on form.
- If youâ€™re new, you can also do a kettlebell deadlift instead of the high pull.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Adjust the rings to a height where you can comfortably reach them while standing.
- **Movement**: 
  - Grab the rings with an overhand grip and lean back, keeping your body straight.
  - Pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together, then lower back down.
  
#### Form Tips:
- Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
- Avoid letting your hips sag or your shoulders round forward.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If rings are too challenging, perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX strap.
- You can also do bent-over dumbbell rows if rings arenâ€™t available.
