## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is designed for quality, focusing on building endurance and strength through a series of movements that will challenge your cardiovascular system and muscular endurance. We’ll be doing 6 rounds of rowing or skiing, followed by box step-ups, kettlebell sumo deadlift high pulls, and ring rows. The goal here is to maintain good form throughout each round, ensuring that we’re not just rushing through the reps but really focusing on quality movement.

## Workout Breakdown

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Start by adjusting the foot straps on the rower or ski erg to secure your feet.
    - For rowing, sit tall with your back straight, and grab the handle with both hands.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - For the ski erg, stand tall, grip the handles, and use a powerful hip hinge to drive your arms down while engaging your core.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your core engaged to protect your back.
    - In rowing, focus on a smooth, controlled motion rather than yanking the handle.
    - In skiing, maintain a fluid motion, using your legs to drive the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you're new to rowing or skiing, consider reducing the time to 2:00 or even 1:30 to focus on form.
    - Alternatively, you can switch to a stationary bike for a similar cardio effect.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box to avoid any slips.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If you have knee issues, consider doing step-ups without weights or even just bodyweight.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out.
    - Place the kettlebell on the ground between your feet.
    - Hinge at your hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels, extending your hips and knees while pulling the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping your elbows high.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back flat and chest up as you hinge.
    - The kettlebell should travel close to your body throughout the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
    - If kettlebells are unavailable, you can substitute with dumbbells or perform a standard deadlift.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to hang with your arms fully extended.
    - Grab the rings and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Avoid letting your hips sag or your shoulders round forward.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If ring rows are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX.
    - Alternatively, you can do bent-over dumbbell rows for a similar effect.

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat:** 
    - Row/Ski: 2:00 max effort
    - Box Step-Ups: 16/8 kg kettlebell
    - Sumo Deadlift High Pulls: 12/6 kg kettlebell
    - Ring Rows: 8 reps

- **Train:** 
    - Row/Ski: 3:00 at a steady pace
    - Box Step-Ups: 20/12 kg kettlebell
    - Sumo Deadlift High Pulls: 20/12 kg kettlebell
    - Ring Rows: 10 reps

- **Compete:** 
    - Row/Ski: 3:00 max effort
    - Box Step-Ups: 24/16 kg kettlebell
    - Sumo Deadlift High Pulls: 24/16 kg kettlebell

### Strategy Advice

As you go through this WOD, remember to pace yourself. It’s easy to get caught up in the intensity, but maintaining quality movement is key. Focus on your breathing, and don’t hesitate to break up the reps if needed, especially on the kettlebell pulls and ring rows. Keep your transitions smooth, and enjoy the workout! Let’s get after it!
