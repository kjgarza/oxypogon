## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Sit comfortably on the rower or ski erg with your feet secured.
- **Rowing**: Start with your legs fully extended, arms straight, and back straight.
- **Pull**: Engage your legs first, then lean back slightly as you pull the handle towards your chest. 
- **Recovery**: Extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back straight and avoid rounding your shoulders.
- Use your legs to drive the movement, not just your arms.
- Maintain a steady rhythm; aim for a consistent stroke rate.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If you're new to rowing, focus on shorter intervals (1:00) or use a lower resistance.
- On the ski erg, reduce the intensity by using a lighter pull.

---

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Stand facing the box with feet hip-width apart.
- **Step Up**: Place one foot firmly on the box, pressing through your heel to lift your body up.
- **Stand Tall**: Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, standing tall.
- **Step Down**: Step back down one foot at a time, maintaining control.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
- Use a box height that allows you to maintain good form without excessive strain.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lower box if youâ€™re new to this movement or have mobility concerns.
- You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on balance and technique.

---

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps, 24/16 kg)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, kettlebell between your feet.
- **Deadlift**: Hinge at your hips and lower your body to grab the kettlebell, keeping your back straight.
- **Pull**: Drive through your heels, extending your hips and knees, and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, leading with your elbows.
- **Lower**: Reverse the movement, lowering the kettlebell back to the ground.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep the kettlebell close to your body throughout the movement.
- Ensure your elbows are higher than your wrists at the top of the pull.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lighter kettlebell if youâ€™re new to this movement or need to focus on form.
- If kettlebells are unavailable, you can substitute with dumbbells.

---

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Setup**: Adjust the rings to a height where you can comfortably reach them while standing.
- **Position**: Grab the rings with an overhand grip and lean back, keeping your body straight.
- **Pull**: Engage your core and pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
- **Lower**: Slowly lower yourself back to the starting position.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
- Avoid letting your hips sag or your shoulders round forward.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If ring rows are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows using a barbell or TRX straps.
- Adjust the angle of your body to make it easier or harder.
