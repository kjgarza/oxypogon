## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is designed for quality, focusing on movement efficiency and endurance. We’ll be going through 6 rounds of rowing or skiing, followed by some box step-ups, kettlebell sumo deadlift high pulls, and finishing off with ring rows. The goal here is to maintain good form and control throughout each movement while keeping a steady pace. Let’s break it down step by step!

---

## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

#### Technique Steps:
- **Rowing**: Sit on the rower with your feet secured in the straps. Start with your knees bent and arms extended. 
  - **Catch Position**: Lean slightly forward, keeping your back straight.
  - **Drive**: Push through your legs, extend your hips, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
  - **Recovery**: Extend your arms first, then hinge at the hips and bend your knees to return to the catch position.
  
- **Ski Erg**: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, holding the handles.
  - **Start Position**: Slightly bend your knees and hinge at the hips.
  - **Pull**: Engage your core and pull the handles down towards your hips, using your arms and legs together.
  - **Return**: Let the handles rise back up while maintaining a slight bend in your knees.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back straight and core engaged to avoid rounding your spine.
- Use your legs to generate power, not just your arms.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or even 1:30.
- Focus on technique rather than speed.

---

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
- Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
- Bring your other foot up to meet the first on top of the box.
- Step back down one foot at a time, maintaining control.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
- Use a box height that allows you to maintain good form without excessive strain.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lower box if you’re new to step-ups or have mobility issues.
- Hold onto a wall or a sturdy object for balance if needed.

---

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out.
- Place the kettlebell between your feet and hinge at your hips to grab the handle.
- **Deadlift**: Push through your heels to lift the kettlebell, keeping it close to your body.
- **High Pull**: As the kettlebell rises, pull your elbows high and wide, bringing it up to your chin level.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your back flat and chest up during the deadlift phase.
- Ensure the kettlebell stays close to your body to maintain control.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re struggling with form.
- Perform the movement without the high pull (just a sumo deadlift) if needed.

---

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

#### Technique Steps:
- Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back comfortably.
- Grab the rings with an overhand grip and walk your feet forward until your body is at an angle.
- Pull your chest towards the rings, keeping your elbows close to your body.
- Lower yourself back down with control.

#### Form Tips:
- Keep your body straight from head to heels; avoid sagging your hips.
- Engage your core throughout the movement.

#### Scaling/Modifications:
- Adjust the height of the rings to make the movement easier or harder.
- If rings are too challenging, perform inverted rows on a barbell or a TRX.

---

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat**: 
  - Row/Ski: 2:00
  - Box Step-Ups: 16/8” box
  - Kettlebell: 12kg/8kg
  - Ring Rows: 8 reps

- **Train**: 
  - Row/Ski: 3:00
  - Box Step-Ups: 20 reps
  - Kettlebell: 16kg/12kg
  - Ring Rows: 10 reps

- **Compete**: 
  - Row/Ski: 3:00
  - Box Step-Ups: 24/16” box
  - Kettlebell: 24kg/16kg
  - Ring Rows: 12 reps

### Strategy Advice:
- Focus on maintaining a steady pace throughout the rounds. It’s better to break up the reps into manageable sets than to go all out and burn out early.
- Prioritize form over speed; quality movements will yield better results in the long run.
