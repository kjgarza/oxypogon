## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is all about quality over quantity, focusing on building endurance and strength through a series of movements that will challenge your cardiovascular system and muscular endurance. We’ll be doing 6 rounds of rowing or skiing, box step-ups, kettlebell sumo deadlift high pulls, and ring rows. The goal here is to maintain good form throughout each movement while keeping a steady pace. Let’s break it down step by step!

---

## Step-by-Step Breakdown

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the rower, sit with your feet strapped in and grab the handle with both hands.
    - Start with your knees bent and your body leaning slightly forward.
    - Drive through your legs, extend your hips, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - Reverse the motion: extend your arms, hinge at the hips, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and core engaged throughout the movement.
    - Focus on a smooth, controlled motion rather than rushing through it.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you're new to rowing, consider reducing the time to 2 minutes or using a lighter resistance on the Ski Erg.
    - You can also alternate with a stationary bike if rowing isn't available.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, then step back down one foot at a time.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core as you step.
    - Use your arms for balance by swinging them naturally as you step.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you're not comfortable with the height.
    - You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on form.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out.
    - Place the kettlebell between your feet and hinge at the hips to grab the handle.
    - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body, leading with your elbows.
    - At the top, your elbows should be above the kettlebell, then lower it back down.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and chest up as you lift.
    - Ensure the kettlebell stays close to your body throughout the movement.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you're new to this movement or focus on the deadlift portion without the high pull.
    - You can also perform the movement with a dumbbell if kettlebells are not available.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended.
    - Grab the rings with an overhand grip and walk your feet forward to create tension.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your body straight, then lower back down.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Engage your core and avoid letting your hips sag.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Adjust the height of the rings to make the movement easier or harder.
    - If rings are not available, you can use a barbell set at waist height for inverted rows.

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat:** 
    - Row/Ski: 2 minutes
    - Box Step-Ups: 16/8 inches
    - Kettlebell: 16/8 kg
    - Ring Rows: 8 reps

- **Train:** 
    - Row/Ski: 3 minutes
    - Box Step-Ups: 20 reps at 20/12 inches
    - Kettlebell: 20/12 kg
    - Ring Rows: 10 reps

- **Compete:** 
    - Row/Ski: 3 minutes
    - Box Step-Ups: 20 reps at 24/16 inches
    - Kettlebell: 24/16 kg
    - Ring Rows: 10 reps

### Strategy Advice

- Focus on maintaining a steady pace throughout the workout rather than going all out in the first round. 
- Break the movements into manageable sets if needed, especially on the kettlebell high pulls and ring rows, to maintain good form.
- Remember, it’s about quality, so prioritize your technique over speed!
