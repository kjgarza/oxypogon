## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is all about quality and consistency. We’re diving into a 6-round workout that combines some cardio, strength, and bodyweight movements. The goal here is to maintain good form throughout each round while focusing on your breathing and pacing. We want to build endurance and strength without sacrificing technique. Let’s break it down!

## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the rower: Sit with your feet strapped in, grab the handle, and start with your knees bent and back straight.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
    - Reverse the motion by extending your arms first, then leaning forward and bending your knees.
    - For the Ski Erg: Stand tall, hold the handles, and initiate with a slight bend in your knees. Pull down with your arms while driving your elbows back, engaging your core.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and avoid rounding your shoulders.
    - Use your legs to drive the movement; your arms should follow.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing, focus on shorter intervals (1:00) or use a lower resistance setting.
    - For the Ski Erg, reduce the distance or time if needed.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand in front of the box with your feet shoulder-width apart.
    - Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - Make sure your entire foot is on the box before stepping down.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - You can also perform step-ups without weights to focus on balance and form.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out.
    - Hold the kettlebell with both hands in front of you, arms extended.
    - Hinge at your hips and lower your body to grab the kettlebell, keeping your back straight.
    - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up to your chin while keeping your elbows high.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep the kettlebell close to your body as you pull.
    - Focus on using your legs and hips to generate power, not just your arms.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
    - If kettlebells are unavailable, you can substitute with dumbbells or perform a sumo deadlift without the high pull.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended.
    - Grab the rings and lean back, keeping your body straight from head to heels.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings by bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body rigid; avoid sagging your hips or arching your back.
    - Focus on pulling with your back, not just your arms.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Adjust the height of the rings to make it easier or harder; lower rings make it easier.
    - If rings are not available, you can perform inverted rows using a barbell or a sturdy table.

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat:** 
    - Row/Ski Erg: 16/8 calories
    - Kettlebell: 12kg/8kg
    - Focus on form and pacing; aim for steady, controlled movements.

- **Train:** 
    - Row/Ski Erg: 20/12 calories
    - Kettlebell: 16kg/12kg
    - Maintain a moderate pace; break up the kettlebell sets if needed.

- **Compete:** 
    - Row/Ski Erg: 24/16 calories
    - Kettlebell: 24kg/16kg
    - Push your limits but keep an eye on your form; aim for unbroken sets if possible.

### Strategy Advice

As you tackle this WOD, remember to focus on quality over quantity. It’s easy to rush through, but maintaining good form will pay off in the long run. Pace yourself, especially on the row/Ski Erg, and break up the kettlebell and step-ups if you start to feel fatigued. Listen to your body, and most importantly, have fun with it! Let’s get after it!
