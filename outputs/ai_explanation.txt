## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is designed for quality, focusing on building endurance and strength through a series of movements that will challenge your cardiovascular system and muscular endurance. We’ll be doing 6 rounds of rowing or skiing, box step-ups, kettlebell sumo deadlift high pulls, and ring rows. The goal here is to maintain good form throughout each movement while keeping a steady pace. Let’s break it down step by step!

---

## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - **Rowing:** Sit on the rower with your feet secured. Grab the handle with both hands, keeping your back straight and core engaged. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest.
    - **Ski Erg:** Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grasp the handles with both hands. Engage your core, and pull the handles down while driving your arms back, using your legs to assist.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and avoid hunching over.
    - Use your legs to initiate the movement; your arms should follow.
    - Maintain a steady rhythm throughout the 3 minutes.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2 minutes or focus on technique at a slower pace.
    - Alternatively, you can substitute with a stationary bike or a brisk walk if equipment is limited.

---

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with feet hip-width apart. Step up onto the box with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Bring your other foot up to the box, then step back down one foot at a time.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core throughout the movement.
    - Ensure your entire foot is on the box to avoid slipping.
    - Alternate the leading foot each round to balance the workload.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - If you have knee issues, consider doing step-ups without weights or perform seated leg lifts instead.

---

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out. Place the kettlebell between your feet.
    - Hinge at the hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands. Engage your core and keep your back flat.
    - Drive through your heels, extend your hips, and pull the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping your elbows high.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Focus on using your legs to lift the kettlebell, not just your arms.
    - Keep the kettlebell close to your body throughout the movement.
    - Control the descent to avoid swinging the kettlebell.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if needed, or perform the movement without weight to focus on form.
    - You can also substitute with dumbbell high pulls if kettlebells are not available.

---

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to lean back with your arms extended. Grab the rings with both hands and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings by bending your elbows and squeezing your shoulder blades together.

- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body straight from head to heels; avoid sagging or arching your back.
    - Engage your core to maintain stability throughout the movement.
    - Control the movement on the way down to maximize strength gains.

- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If rings are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX.
    - Alternatively, do bent-over dumbbell rows for a similar effect.

---

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Train:**

    - Box Step-Ups: 20 inches
    - Kettlebell: 20/12 kg
    - Ring Rows: 10 reps

- **Compete:** 
    - Row/Ski: 3 minutes
    - Box Step-Ups: 24 inches
    - Kettlebell: 24/16 kg
    - Ring Rows: 10 reps

### Strategy Advice

- Focus on maintaining a steady pace throughout the workout. It’s better to break up the reps into manageable sets than to go all out and burn out quickly.
- Keep your transitions smooth between movements to save time and maintain your heart rate.
- Remember to hydrate and listen to your body; if something feels off, don’t hesitate to scale back or modify as needed.
