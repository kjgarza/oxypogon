## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is all about quality over quantity, focusing on building endurance and strength through a series of movements that will challenge your cardiovascular system and muscular endurance. We’ll be doing 6 rounds of rowing or skiing, followed by box step-ups, kettlebell sumo deadlift high pulls, and ring rows. The goal here is to maintain good form and a steady pace throughout each round, ensuring that we’re not just rushing through the reps but really focusing on our technique and control.

## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - For the Row: Sit on the rower, strap your feet in, and grab the handle with both hands. Start with your knees bent and your body leaning slightly forward.
    - Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle to your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body.
    - Reverse the motion: extend your arms, lean forward, and bend your knees to return to the starting position.
    - For the Ski Erg: Stand tall with the handles at your waist. Pull the handles down towards your hips while engaging your core and using your legs to assist.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and core engaged throughout the movement.
    - Avoid overextending your arms; focus on using your legs and core to drive the movement.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If you’re new to rowing or skiing, reduce the time to 2:00 or 1:30 to focus on form.
    - Consider using a lower resistance setting on the Ski Erg if you’re struggling to maintain pace.

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand facing the box with your feet hip-width apart.
    - Step up onto the box with one foot, driving through your heel to lift your body up.
    - Bring your other foot up to meet the first on the box, then step back down one foot at a time.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your chest up and engage your core to maintain balance.
    - Step all the way up, ensuring your entire foot is on the box before stepping down.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lower box height if you’re not comfortable with the height.
    - Perform step-ups to a sturdy platform or use a bench if a box isn’t available.

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width apart, toes slightly pointed out. Place the kettlebell between your feet.
    - Hinge at your hips and bend your knees to grab the kettlebell with both hands.
    - Drive through your heels to stand up, pulling the kettlebell up along your body to your chin, elbows high.
    - Lower the kettlebell back down to the ground and repeat.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your back straight and chest up during the lift.
    - Use your legs to initiate the movement, not just your arms.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
    - If kettlebells are not available, you can substitute with dumbbells or perform a bodyweight squat.

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

- **Technique Steps:**
    - Set the rings at a height that allows you to pull your body up while keeping your feet on the ground.
    - Grab the rings with an overhand grip and lean back, keeping your body straight.
    - Pull your chest towards the rings, squeezing your shoulder blades together, then lower back down.
  
- **Form Tips:**
    - Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels.
    - Avoid using momentum; focus on a controlled pull and lower.
  
- **Scaling/Modifications:**
    - If the rings are too challenging, perform inverted rows on a bar or use a TRX strap.
    - You can also perform bodyweight rows on a low bar or table.

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat:** 
    - Use a 16/8 kg kettlebell for the high pulls, and aim for a lower box height for step-ups.
  
- **Train:** 
    - Use a 20/12 kg kettlebell, and maintain a standard box height.
  
- **Compete:** 
    - Use a 24/16 kg kettlebell, and challenge yourself with a higher box if you’re comfortable.

### Strategy Advice

As you move through this WOD, remember that quality is key. Focus on maintaining good form throughout each movement, and don’t rush through the rounds. It’s better to take a few extra seconds to ensure you’re executing each rep correctly than to sacrifice form for speed. Break up the reps if needed, especially on the kettlebell high pulls and ring rows, to maintain your technique. Let’s get after it, and have some fun!
