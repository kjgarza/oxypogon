## Workout Overview

Hey everyone! Today’s WOD is all about building endurance and strength while keeping our form sharp. We’ll be doing 6 rounds of a mix of rowing or skiing, box step-ups, kettlebell sumo deadlift high pulls, and ring rows. The goal here is to focus on quality movement, so take your time and really dial in your technique. This workout is designed to challenge your cardiovascular fitness, lower body strength, and upper body pulling power. Let’s break it down!

---

## Workout Components

### 1. Row/Ski Erg (3:00)

**Technique Steps:**
- **Rowing:** Sit on the rower with your feet strapped in. Start with your knees bent and your arms extended. Push through your legs, lean back slightly, and pull the handle towards your chest, keeping your elbows close.
- **Ski Erg:** Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding the handles. Start with your arms extended overhead, then pull down while bending your knees slightly, engaging your core.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your back straight and core engaged throughout the movement.
- For rowing, focus on driving with your legs first, then leaning back and pulling with your arms.
- For the Ski Erg, maintain a fluid motion, using your legs to assist your arms.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- If you’re new to rowing, consider using a lower damper setting for an easier pull.
- For Ski Erg, reduce the time to 2:00 if needed.

---

### 2. Box Step-Ups (20 reps)

**Technique Steps:**
- Stand in front of the box with your feet hip-width apart. 
- Step up with one foot, pressing through your heel to lift your body onto the box.
- Bring your other foot up to meet the first, then step back down one foot at a time.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your chest up and core tight as you step.
- Use your legs to lift, not your back. Ensure your entire foot is on the box before standing up.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- Use a lower box if you’re not comfortable with the height.
- If you have knee issues, consider doing step-ups without weights or using a bench.

---

### 3. Kettlebell Sumo Deadlift High Pulls (15 reps)

**Technique Steps:**
- Stand with your feet wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly pointed out. Place the kettlebell between your feet.
- Hinge at your hips to grab the kettlebell with both hands. 
- Drive through your heels, extending your hips and knees while pulling the kettlebell up to your chin, keeping your elbows high.

**Form Tips:**
- Keep your back flat and chest up as you lift.
- The kettlebell should travel close to your body; think about keeping it in a straight line.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- Use a lighter kettlebell if you’re new to this movement.
- If kettlebells aren’t available, you can substitute with dumbbells or perform a bodyweight squat.

---

### 4. Ring Rows (10 reps)

**Technique Steps:**
- Set the rings to a height where you can comfortably reach them while standing. 
- Grab the rings with an overhand grip, lean back, and keep your body straight.
- Pull your chest towards the rings while keeping your elbows close to your body.

**Form Tips:**
- Maintain a straight line from your head to your heels; avoid sagging your hips.
- Focus on squeezing your shoulder blades together as you pull.

**Scaling/Modifications:**
- If ring rows are too challenging, you can perform inverted rows on a barbell or use a TRX.
- For a more accessible option, do seated rows with a resistance band.

---

## Loading and Rep-Modification Options

- **Sweat:** 
  - Row/Ski: 16/8 calories
  - Box Step-Ups: Bodyweight or low box
  - Kettlebell: 12kg/8kg
  - Ring Rows: 5-7 reps

- **Train:** 
  - Row/Ski: 20/12 calories
  - Box Step-Ups: Moderate height box
  - Kettlebell: 16kg/12kg
  - Ring Rows: 8-10 reps

- **Compete:** 
  - Row/Ski: 24/16 calories
  - Box Step-Ups: Higher box with weights
  - Kettlebell: 24kg/16kg
  - Ring Rows: 10-12 reps

### Strategy Advice

As you tackle this WOD, remember to pace yourself! Focus on maintaining good form rather than rushing through the reps. Break up the movements if you need to, especially as fatigue sets in. Keep your transitions smooth and stay hydrated. Let’s have fun and crush this workout together!
